RSA (Rivest-Shamir-Adleman) is an asymmetric encryption and decryption method, meaning it uses two different keys: public and private. The public key is used for encryption and is known to everyone, while the private key is used for decryption and must be kept secret by the receiver. Key generation requires the choosing of two large prime numbers (p and q). These primes are to be kept secret. N or modulus N, and N resembles the calculated product of p and q, making N your public key. Next, Euler’s Totient Function is calculated as φ(N) = (p − 1)(q − 1). A public exponent e is then selected such that it is greater than 1 and relatively prime to φ(N). The public key consists of the pair (N, e). A private exponent d is then calculated so that (d × e) mod φ(N) = 1. The private key consists of the pair (N, d). To encrypt a message, the plaintext must be converted to numerical values. The encryption formula for RSA is C=M^e mod N (note M is the plaintext, and C is your ciphertext). The decryption formula for RSA is M = C^d mod N (note C resembles the ciphertext and M resembles the plaintext).
